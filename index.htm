Archiving YOUR WORK
(10minutes)

Transferring your domain away from wix will remove your current website as you have designed it from being available to view online.

To avoid losing the work you have put into your design, i encourage making a local copy of the documents, image files, and links used before starting the domain transfer.

This might look like,

  • Organizing logo, image, video, and/or audio files and labelling them according to their website organization
  • Copy/pasting accompanying text and externally linked websites and files in a locally saved document
  • Noting any colour codes and font choices you may want to use in the future
  • Consider taking screenshots and screen recordings of your website for reference

Though this step isn't necessary it might be helpful when you start buidling your website on another platform.

>
WIX FINE PRINT

Your domain registered with wix will only be taken offline after you officially 'Transfer Away from Wix' and use the authorization code provided to confirm this transfer ...more on this later

As soon as the domain transfer process is started, your official website (under the wix premium subscription) will be taken offline. Unfortunately, wix does not allow users to delete their account and instead defaults it back to the 'free' version, meaning that your website will remain online at the wix URL [accountname.wixsite.com/siteaddress] until the end of your subscription period.

It is unlikely that anyone will be searching for your website using this wix specific URL, but i would encourage removing all of your work (after saving locally) from your wix account so that is no longer available for wix to host.

transferring your domain

NOTE

Your custom domain www.name.com may include or exclude the www. and end in a variety of ways, .com being the most common. 

>
Where did you originally purchase ur Domain ?
(10minutes)

If you purchased your domain several years ago and don't remember where, use whois.com to search for your domain name registrar. Some popular examples being GoDaddy, BlueHost, DreamHost, NameCheap, Google Domains, Network Solutions...

After you've figured out where you purchased ur domain, you will need to login and/or troubleshoot logging in to get access to your account. This might require getting in touch with the customer services.

OR

If wix is your first website ever, it is very likely you purchased your custom domain directly from wix. In this case, you will need to choose a domain name register (mentioned above) to transfer your wix domain to. I recommend looking through the domain registrar options and read some reviews-- the transfer process is free but registrar's will charge you a one-year extension of your domain. The pricing of which will vary slightly across domain registrars and on your domain name (for instance if it ends in .com or .net or .org this will influence the cost). You can find an approximate cost for each domain registrar by searching "domain transfer fee [name of domain registrar]" but it should be less than $30 a year.

OR

If you don't have a custom domain and are using wix under the free user subscription, you don't have to worry about this! Contact me if you would like to purchase a domain and connect it to one of these templates (but this is not necessary)

You should receive a code from wix.com at the email associated with your wix account. This code needs to be entered at your domain name registrar. See these detailed steps according to your registrar, . If you have ur domain at another platform, search "Transfer domain to [name of domain registrar]", it should be relatively straightforward.

Remember that if you purchased your domain directly from wix you will need to pay a "transfer fee". In any case, DO NOT purchase any hosting add ons, this is entirely unnecessary!
